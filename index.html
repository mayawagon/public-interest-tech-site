The Technology & Social Change Project

This project is intended to find out who works in public interest technology, how they got there, and what needs to be done to support this kind of work. Public interest technology is “any use of technology in the public interest,” and it is a rapidly growing field. It includes people working within government, the private sector, nonprofits, and community-based organizations. Research Action Design (RAD) and the Open Technology Institute (OTI) are conducting a community-led field scan of public interest technology that centers diverse practitioners and identifies challenges and opportunities. The field scan will identify people, groups, institutions, and networks who are already working on public interest technology, and explore how best to foster the growth of the field. This is a participatory research project that will be guided by a group of practitioners who are deeply engaged in the work, from diverse perspectives. The project is funded by NetGain, and is meant to inform NetGain’s philanthropy.
